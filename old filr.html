Faceted Forms

Lexicon & Nomenclature

01 — Primitives

Every form begins as a circle. Three geometric modifications produce distinct primitive types.

02 — Connections

When two same-color circles come within gap range (0.65× minR), a connection is computed. Two types exist, chosen per pair at generation time (~70% rod bar probability).

Fillet Neck Anatomy

Four arcs: two outer arcs (back of each circle) and two concave fillet arcs (the bridge). The fillet circle radius determines neck thickness: Rf = max(Ra×0.55, gap×0.5 + (Ra+Rb)×0.22).

Rod Bar Anatomy

Common external tangent lines touch both circles simultaneously. φ = lineAng ± acos((Rb−Ra)/dist). Contact point = center − R×(cosφ, sinφ). Min bar width enforced at 55% of smaller radius.

04 — Physics
TermDefinition
AttractForce pulling each shape toward canvas center. Controls composition tightness.
RepelInter-circle push force within 2× touching distance. Velocity-level separation.
DampenVelocity multiplier per frame (0.7–0.98). Lower = more friction.
Same-color attractAdditional pull between circles sharing a color. Creates clusters that form blobs and rods.
Positional correction6 iterations per frame of direct position pushing. Resolves all circle-circle overlap. Mass-weighted.
KEKinetic energy = Σ(v²×mass). Bake stops when KE < 0.12.
BakeRun physics until rest. Auto-triggers on Generate. Same-color shapes cluster and form connections.
05 — Output Formats
FormatDescription
Solid SVGFlat-fill vector. Per-shape <clipPath> contains fills. Washer holes as <mask> knockouts. Rod bars as polygon fills.
Plotter SVGStroke-only for AxiDraw. One Inkscape layer per color. Arc segments clipped by occlusion testing. Rod bars as two tangent stroke lines.
PNG Proof2× raster with seed/date metadata strip.
Ray SVGBlack background. Shape silhouettes punched as voids. Spiral fills radiate outward from each shape center.
